📊 How this tool works

🎯 Purpose

Analyze the relationship between air pollution (PM₂.₅, NO₂, O₃, etc.) and epidemiological incidence rates. Explore delayed effects (lag), detect extreme incidence peaks, and visualize seasonal trends.

📁 Data requirements

  • date (YYYY-MM-DD) and incidencia_1000 are mandatory.
  • Optional: pm2.5, pm10, no2, nox, o3, so2.
  • CSV format with header, commas or semicolons supported.

🚀 How to use

  1. Select a preloaded city dataset or upload your own CSV using "Browse".
  2. Adjust time filters to focus on specific periods.
  3. Explore lagged correlations (0–7 days) for delayed impact.
  4. WHO Relative Risk: By default, this is not calculated. You must change the pollutant and lag to trigger it. Once active, it calculates the risk and provides the overall means and day counts for both high-exposure and low-exposure scenarios.
  5. Peaks table shows statistically unusual incidence days.
  6. Monthly bar chart reveals seasonal patterns.

📈 Correlation & p-values

Pearson's r + significance. p < 0.05 indicates association. Lag analysis reveals delayed effects.

⚠️ Peak detection

Threshold = mean + 2×standard deviation. Helps identify outbreak episodes.

🩺 WHO Relative Risk

Triggered by changing pollutants/lags. Splits data based on WHO thresholds to provide incidence means for both scenarios.
